About

I was born and raised in East Germany. At the age of 15 I started to learn the guitar, sing in the choir and spent hours figuring out how the internet was build and how to make use of it. I spent a year in Utah where I made my first object oriented programming experiences and build my first applications and platforms in PHP. Event calendars, self-made databases, a CMS in PHP and MySQL, sort of a myspace clone and funky battle chat concepts. I decided to study visual communications at Bauhaus-University Weimar and graduated with a diploma on "Cultural Presets". During that long and extraordinary time I explored what is possible outside of the digital world, abandoned coding for a while, turned to semiotics but eventually returned to my roots. I set out to challenge my ideas in the real world. What interests me is the cognitive in-between different states or definitions that we create collectively as a culture and those that we make for ourselves - subconscious communication or what can not be communicated by itself, yet. To research this and expand my understanding of this I produce art shows, media and internet artworks, performances and events. Therefore I also dedicated my professional career to interfaces, the in between human and machines, and or between machines amongst themselves.
